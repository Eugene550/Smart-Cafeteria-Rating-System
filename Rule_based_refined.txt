SMART CAFETERIA RATING SYSTEM — REFINED FUZZY RULE BASE
========================================================

Conventions (read first):
- Four input variables, all scored so that HIGH = GOOD:
    Food Quality, Cleanliness, Menu Variety, Service Speed.
- Service Speed was renamed from "Waiting Time" to keep the polarity intuitive:
    High = quick / short wait (good), Low = slow / long queue (bad).
- "Unknown" has been removed. It is not a fuzzy level (it means "no data"), so if a
    review does not mention an aspect, default that aspect to Medium BEFORE inference.
- Rule 17 is a low-priority DEFAULT so that no input combination is ever left unrated.
- Levels: Low / Medium / High.  Output: Overall Rating = Low / Medium / High.

| Rule | IF (Conditions)                                                                  | THEN (Rating) |
| ---- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------- |
|  1   | Food High AND Cleanliness High AND Menu Variety High AND Service Speed High      | High          |
|  2   | Food High AND Cleanliness High AND Service Speed High                            | High          |
|  3   | Food High AND Cleanliness High AND Menu Variety Medium AND Service Speed High    | High          |
|  4   | Food High AND Menu Variety High AND Service Speed High                           | High          |
|  5   | Food High AND Cleanliness Medium AND Menu Variety Medium AND Service Speed Medium| High          |
|  6   | Food Medium AND Cleanliness Medium AND Menu Variety Medium AND Service Speed Medium | Medium     |
|  7   | Food Medium AND Cleanliness High AND Menu Variety Medium AND Service Speed Medium| Medium        |
|  8   | Food High AND Service Speed Low                                                  | Medium        |
|  9   | Food High AND Cleanliness Medium AND Menu Variety High AND Service Speed Low     | Medium        |
|  10  | Cleanliness High AND Menu Variety Low AND Service Speed High                     | Medium        |
|  11  | Food High AND Menu Variety Low AND Service Speed Low                             | Medium        |
|  12  | Food Medium AND Cleanliness Medium AND Menu Variety High AND Service Speed High  | Medium        |
|  13  | Food Medium AND Cleanliness Medium AND Menu Variety Medium AND Service Speed Low | Medium        |
|  14  | Cleanliness High AND Menu Variety Low AND Service Speed Low                      | Medium        |
|  15  | Food Medium AND Cleanliness Medium AND Menu Variety Low AND Service Speed Low    | Low           |
|  16  | Food Low OR Cleanliness Low                                                      | Low           |
|  17  | (Default) any combination not strongly matched above                            | Medium        |

Notes on the rule base:
- Rules 1-5 cover the strong/positive region (something is genuinely good).
- Rules 6-14 cover the common mixed / average cases.
- LOW region (rules 15-16):
    * Rule 16 is the VETO rule: bad food OR poor cleanliness forces Low regardless of
      the other aspects (an OR rule, fires on either condition). Because it is an OR,
      it already absorbs EVERY case where food or cleanliness is Low.
    * Rule 15 covers the case the veto misses: nothing is actually good (no High),
      food and cleanliness are only passable (Medium), AND the two remaining aspects
      are both bad (Menu Variety Low, Service Speed Low). Previously this drifted to
      the default Medium, which was too generous.
- Two changes were made so Low is reached fairly without padding:
    * Rule 11 was TIGHTENED (added "Food High") so a clearly weak cafeteria is no
      longer rescued to Medium just because variety and speed are poor.
    * Rule 14 was ADDED so that genuinely clean cafeterias (Cleanliness High) with
      poor variety and slow service still land on Medium, not Low.
- Why only two Low rules: rule 16's OR veto absorbs all Food-Low / Cleanliness-Low
    cases, so the only remaining genuine Low scenario is rule 15. Adding more Low
    rules would duplicate the veto (e.g. "Food Low AND Service Speed Low" is pointless
    because Food Low alone already fires rule 16). Every rule here maps to a scenario
    you can state out loud.
- Rule 17 (default) is implemented in code as the fallback consequent — e.g. a
    low-weight "Rating is Medium" rule — so that an input matching no other rule still
    produces an output. This prevents "dead zones" in a Mamdani system.
- You have 17 rules, not the full 3^4 = 81 combinations. A Mamdani FIS does not need
    every combination because rules fire PARTIALLY (by degree of match); a curated set
    plus the veto and default rules covers the input space.
